A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, renowned as the "Missile Man of India," was a visionary scientist and the 11th President of India. He played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. Beyond his scientific achievements, Kalam was a celebrated educator and inspirational figure, known for his simple lifestyle and motivational speeches that resonated with people of all ages. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam's journey from humble beginnings to the highest office in the nation is a testament to his dedication and perseverance. He began his career at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). At ISRO, he contributed significantly to the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, marking a significant milestone in the country's space program. Kalam's most notable contributions came in the field of missile technology. He played a crucial role in the development of Agni and Prithvi missiles, earning him the moniker "Missile Man of India".

Biographies

  • Full name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul kalam
  • Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • Came from a poor but disciplined and educated Muslim family
  • Had a deep interest in science and mathematics since childhood
  • Studied aeronautical engineering at Madras Institute of Technology (MIT)
  • Joined DRDO and ISRO and contributed to India’s missile development
  • Known as the “Missile Man of India” for his work on Agni and Prithvi missiles
  • Played a major role in India's 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests
  • Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007
  • Nicknamed the “People’s President” for his humble and approachable nature
